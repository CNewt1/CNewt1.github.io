Impact animal extinction can have on ecosystems1

Animal extinction has widespread cascading effects for any ecosystem the extinct organism inhabited. Losing an organism, whether it be plant life or animal life, has an effect on all other organisms that have any amount of contact with the extinct organism, whether it be direct or indirect contact. Extinction is a natural occurrence, in fact 99% of all species that ever existed are now extinct. However; the rate of which species are going extinct is and has been accelerating at an alarming rate due to human activity.source

This site breifly focuses on a few endangered species of big cat, but these four species aren't even a drop in the bucket compared to the tens of thousands of currently endangered species, if you would like to read more you can find information here and here.

Current number of endangered species2

There are over 37,400 species in danger of extinction according to IUCN's Red List of Threatened Species

  • 41 percent of amphibians are endangered
  • 34 percent of conifers are endangered
  • 33 percent of reed building corals are endangered
  • 26 percent of mammals are endangered
  • 14 percent of birds are endangered

Effects of over-hunting1

A well known example of the effects of over-hunting are the wolves of Yellowstone Park who were hunted to near-extinction by the year 1930. With the wolves nearly wiped out the prey animals, such as deer, were able to thrive and multiplied rapidly. The increased number of deer and elk were able to ravage the native plant species which were home to many song birds, which continued on a chain reaction allowing pesty insects like mosquitoes (which the birds would have normally eaten) to over run the park.
